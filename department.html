Anaesthesiology

Anaesthesiology, is a branch of medicine that allows safe usage of medical drugs to induce a reversible state of total or partial lack of responsiveness to surgical procedures, so that they can be carried out smoothly, without causing any distress and inconvenience to the patient

The Department of Anaesthesiology at CARE NOW Hospital uses the most advanced, safe and patient-focused techniques in the delivery of anaesthetic care. Our team of extremely skilled and well-experienced.

Neurologist

A neurologist treats disorders that affect the brain, spinal cord and nerves. When you're facing serious conditions like stroke, Alzheimer's disease, multiple sclerosis, or ALS, it's critical to find the right doctor for you. Your brain and its memory function depend on good blood flow and healthy nerves to work well. Your doctor might refer you to a neurologist if you're having symptoms that could be caused by a neurological condition, such as pain, memory loss, trouble with balance, or tremors.

Cardiology

The field includes medical diagnosis and treatment of congenital heart defects, coronary artery disease, heart failure, valvular heart disease and electrophysiology. Physicians who specialize in this field of medicine are called cardiologists, a specialty of internal medicine.

Education required: Doctor of Medicine (M.D.)

Activity sectors: Medicine, Surgery

Names: Physician, Surgeon

Pulmonology

Pulmonology is an area of medicine that focuses on the health of the respiratory system. Pulmonologists treat everything from asthma to tuberculosis. Find out the educational requirements for specializing in this area of medicine, the conditions pulmonologists treat, and when you should see one.

the respiratory system includes the organs that help you breathe. The three major parts of this system are the airway, the lungs, and the respiratory muscles.

Ophthalmology

“Ophthalmology is a discipline of medicine which deals with the physiology, anatomy and diseases of the eye”. Ophthalmology is “The Science of Eyes”. ... A doctor who specializes in surgical and medical eye problems are generally termed as “Ophthalmologists”.

An ophthalmologist is a medical or osteopathic doctor who specializes in eye and vision care. Ophthalmologists differ from optometrists and opticians in their levels of training and in what they can diagnose and treat.
